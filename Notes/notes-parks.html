National Parks FAQ

How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2018: 318,211,833.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: More than 20,000
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.

Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.

Glacier National Park

  • Location and Size: Glacier National Park is located in the U.S. state of Montana, along the border with Canada. It covers an area of over 1 million acres (4,000 square kilometers) and is part of the larger Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Glaciers and Peaks: The park is known for its stunning alpine landscapes, rugged mountains, and numerous glaciers. However, due to climate change, the number of glaciers in the park has decreased significantly over the years. The park is home to towering peaks, including the famous Going-to-the-Sun Mountain.
  • Going-to-the-Sun Road: This iconic road is a engineering marvel that cuts through the heart of the park, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The road spans approximately 50 miles (80 kilometers) and crosses the Continental Divide at Logan Pass.
  • Biodiversity: Despite its harsh climate and high elevation, Glacier National Park is home to diverse ecosystems. It provides habitat for a variety of wildlife, including grizzly bears, mountain goats, bighorn sheep, and more. The park's ecosystems range from subalpine forests to alpine tundra.

Lassen Volcanic National Park

  • Volcanic Wonders: Lassen Volcanic National Park is located in Northern California, USA, and is known for its remarkable volcanic activity. It features a variety of geothermal features such as hot springs, fumaroles, and boiling mud pots, showcasing the area's volcanic history.
  • Lassen Peak: The park's namesake, Lassen Peak, is one of the world's largest plug dome volcanoes and the southernmost volcano in the Cascade Range. It last erupted between 1914 and 1917, and its explosive activity significantly altered the landscape.
  • Bumpass Hell: Bumpass Hell is one of the park's most famous hydrothermal areas. Visitors can hike to this area to see colorful and steaming hot springs, as well as the iconic turquoise Bumpass Hell pond.
  • Wildlife and Nature: Lassen National Park is home to diverse flora and fauna, ranging from coniferous forests to alpine meadows. Wildlife in the park includes black bears, mule deer, and a variety of bird species. The park offers numerous hiking trails that allow visitors to explore these natural wonders.

Arches National Park

  • Unique Sandstone Arches: Arches National Park, located in southeastern Utah, USA, is renowned for its more than 2,000 natural stone arches. These arches are formed by erosion over millions of years and come in various sizes and shapes, making the park a captivating geological wonder.
  • Delicate Arch: One of the park's most iconic landmarks is Delicate Arch, an isolated arch that has become a symbol of Utah. It's a popular spot for hikers and photographers, and it's particularly stunning during sunrise and sunset.
  • Landscape and Erosion: The park's landscape is dominated by striking red rock formations, fins, and balanced rocks. The unique geological processes that have shaped the area include erosion, weathering, and the natural arch formation that the park is named after.
  • Ancient History: While the park is known for its natural features, it also holds a rich cultural history. The area was inhabited by various Native American cultures over thousands of years. Petroglyphs and other evidence of their presence can be found throughout the park.
